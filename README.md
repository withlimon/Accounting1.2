# Introduction to Accounting
 * Origin: Count \rightarrow Account
 * 1494, Luca Pacioli: Known as the Father of Accounting. He was the first writer on modern accounting.
 * Double Entry System: Focuses on Debit and Credit. (লেনদেন লিপিবদ্ধ করার সিস্টেম)
 * Financial Year (FY): 1st July 2025 – 30th June 2026.
 * Transaction: Involves a minimum of two persons.
 * Rule: #All events are not transactions, but all transactions are events.
Key Abbreviations & Reference Books
 * AICPA: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants.
 * FASB: Financial Accounting Standard Board.
 * SEC: Security and Exchange Commission.
 * DSE: Dhaka Stock Exchange.
 * CSE: Chittagong Stock Exchange.
Reference Books:
 * Basic Accounting – S.A. Hasib & S.E. Kabir
 * Accounting Principles – Weygand, Kieso, and Kimmel
Definitions of Accounting
> "Accounting is the art of recording, classifying and summarizing in a significant manner and in terms of money, transactions and events which are in part at least of a financial character and interpreting results thereof." — AICPA
> 
> "Accounting is a process used to measure and report to various users relevant financial information regarding the economic activities of an organization or unit."
> 
Users of Accounting Information
 * Owner of the business/firm
 * Management Authority
 * Government
 * Potential Investors
 * Tax Authority
 * Price Fixation Authority
 * Trade Union / Labour Union / Association
 * Economic Planner
 * Researchers
 * In general public
Types of Business Ownership
 * Sole Tradership Business
 * Partnership Business: (2 to 20 partners)
 * Joint Stock Company (PLC):
   * Public Limited Company: Profit/loss is shared based on the amount invested.
   * Sleeping Partner: No investment but gets a share of the profit.
##  **Accounting Concepts & Principles**

| No. | Principle / Concept | Definition & Key Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Entity Concept** | The business and the owner are treated as separate legal entities. |
| **2** | **Dual Aspect** | Every transaction has two sides: **Debit** and **Credit**. |
| **3** | **Going Concern** | ব্যবসায় চলমান রেখে হিসাব করা (Assumption that business will continue). |
| **4** | **Historical Cost** | Records transactions at original cost (Economic History). |
| **5** | **Periodical Principle** | নির্দিষ্ট সময়ে আয়-ব্যয় (Financial reporting for specific periods). |
| **6** | **Matching Principle** | Comparing and matching income against expenses. |
| **7** | **Realisation Principle** | আদায়করণ নীতি (Revenue is recognized when earned). |
| **8** | **Disclosure Principle** | নির্দিষ্ট সময় পর পর update প্রকাশ করা (Full disclosure). |
| **9** | **Materiality Principle** | Focuses on the significance of items / "lasting of products." |
| **10** | **Consistency** | সমরূপতা (Uniformity in methods over different periods). |
| **11** | **Conservation** | রক্ষণশীল নীতি (Prudence: Anticipate no profits, provide for all losses). |
| **12** | **Legislation / Legal** | Compliance with relevant laws and regulations. |

---

### **Key Rules & Categorization**

* **Accounting Conventions:** Items 10, 11, and 12 (Consistency, Conservation, and Legislation) are specifically grouped as **Conventions**.
* **The Prudence Rule:** In **Conservation**, the lower price (Cost or Market) will be taken, not the market price.
* **Maximum Accounting Time:** 12 months.
* **Depreciation:** Applied to fixed assets (অবচয়).
* 
Important Notes on Principles:
 * Convention: Consistency, Conservation, and Legislation are categorized as Conventions.
 * Maximum Accounting Time: 12 months.
 * Conservation Rule: Lower price will be taken, not the market price (Lower of Cost or Market).
 * Depreciation: Calculated on fixed assets (অবচয়).
 * DPS: Deposit Pension Skill Scheme.


## 1. Classification of Account
There are five primary classifications of accounts:

1.  **Asset** (Divided into *Current assets* and *Fixed assets*)
2.  **Liability**
3.  **Expenditure**
4.  **Income**
5.  **Capital** $\rightarrow$ Internal liability

> **Note:** Cash Taka is a **liquid asset**.

---

## 2. Detailed Breakdown of Accounts

### **A. Assets**
* **Current Assets:** Cash, bank balance, accounts debtors/receivable, notes receivable, prepaid expenses, short-term investment, stock goods (মজুদ পণ্য).
    * *On account* = বাকিতে
    * *Closing stock* = যে প্রোডাক্ট বিক্রি হয়নি
    * *Starting stock*
* **Fixed Assets:** Land, gold, furniture, machine, building, long-term investment, **Goodwill** (অদৃশ্যমান সম্পদ).

### **B. Liability (দায়)**
* **Current Liability:** Short term (ধারে, কয়েকদিনের মধ্যে পরিশোধ).
    * Includes: Accounts payable/creditor, note payable, bank overdraft, unpaid expenses, short-term loan.
* **Long-term Liability:** Housing loan, bank loan (কিস্তি), debenture, mortgage loan (বন্ধক).

### **C. Expenditure**
* **Operating Expenses:** Salary, rent, advertisement, utilities (regular expenses).

---

## 3. Rules for Debit and Credit of Accounts

| No. | Account Type | Increase | Decrease |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Asset** | Debit | Credit |
| 2 | **Liability** | Credit | Debit |
| 3 | **Expenditure** | Debit | Credit |
| 4 | **Income** | Credit | Debit |
| 5 | **Capital** | Credit | Debit |

---

## 4. Accounting Cycle
The accounting cycle follows a continuous flow (ঘটনা ঘটার সাথে সাথে লিপিবদ্ধ করা):

1.  **Journal** (Primary entry)
2.  **Ledger** (Permanent storage of an account)
3.  **Trial Balance** (Schedule of all ledgers)
4.  **Adjustment**
5.  **Final Account / Financial Statement** (F/S)
6.  **Analysis and Interpretation of F/S**

## 5. The Accounting Equation
$$A = L + E$$
* **A = Asset**
* **L = Liability**
* **E = Owner's Equity / Capital**

**Effect on Capital:**
* Expenditure / Loss $\rightarrow$ Capital decreases.
* Income / Profit $\rightarrow$ Capital increases.
